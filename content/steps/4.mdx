<SafetyCallout variant="warning" title="No Universal Dose">
  Peptide dosing is not standardized for human use. Doses referenced in this guide are drawn from research literature and community experience — not from FDA-approved labeling. Always start conservatively and adjust based on your individual response.
</SafetyCallout>

## Units of Measurement

Peptide doses are typically measured in **micrograms (mcg)** or **milligrams (mg)**:

- **1 mg = 1,000 mcg**
- Most peptide doses are in the **100–500 mcg** range per injection
- GLP-1 class peptides are typically dosed in **mg** per week

Understanding this conversion is essential before you calculate draw volumes. Confusing mcg and mg is a serious dosing error — a 1,000× difference.

## Common Dosing Ranges by Goal

These are illustrative ranges from research and community protocols. They are not prescriptions, and doses vary significantly by individual and peptide.

| Goal | Typical Dose Range | Typical Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Tissue repair | 250–500 mcg | Once or twice daily |
| Growth hormone / body composition | 100–200 mcg | Once or twice daily (pulsed) |
| Bioregulators (short course) | 1–10 mg | Course-based, 2–4× per year |
| Immune modulation | 1–1.5 mg | Twice weekly |
| Sexual health | 1–2 mg | As needed, max 2×/week |
| GLP-1 / metabolic (e.g. Retatrutide) | Start low, titrate | Once weekly |

<SafetyCallout variant="info" title="Start Low, Go Slow">
  Regardless of the protocol you have read, begin at the low end of the dose range on your first injection. Assess your response over 24–48 hours before increasing. This applies especially to GLP-1 class peptides where nausea and GI distress are common at higher doses.
</SafetyCallout>

## Dosing Frequency

How often you inject depends on the peptide's half-life and your goals:

- **Short half-life peptides** — typically dosed once or twice daily
- **Long half-life / depot peptides** — typically once weekly
- **GLP-1 agonists** — once weekly, titrate up slowly
- **Short course protocols** — defined courses with off periods

Missing a dose occasionally is generally not a problem. Do not double-dose to catch up.

## Cycle Lengths and Breaks

Most peptide protocols involve defined cycles with rest periods:

- **BPC-157, TB-500**: 4–12 week protocols, then assess
- **CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin**: 3–6 month protocols are common, with breaks of 4–8 weeks
- **GLP-1 class**: typically used until goal is reached, then maintenance dose
- **Epitalon**: 10–20 day course, 2–4× per year

Continuous, indefinite use without breaks is generally not recommended, as receptor desensitization and downregulation of natural signaling pathways are concerns with many of these compounds.
