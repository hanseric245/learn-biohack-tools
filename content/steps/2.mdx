## Sourcing Peptides

Peptides exist in a regulatory gray area in most countries. They are sold as "for research use only, not for human consumption" — but they are widely used by individuals who self-administer.

Use a trusted vendor. While certification is difficult to verify independently, reputation in the community carries significant weight. Ask someone you trust for a referral before purchasing.

## Supplies You Will Need

Beyond the peptide itself, you need the following before you can proceed safely.

### Reconstitution Supplies

| Item | Purpose | Where to Find |
|---|---|---|
| Bacteriostatic water (BW) | Solvent for reconstitution | Medical supply or compounding pharmacy |
| Alcohol swabs | Sterilize vial tops and injection sites | Pharmacy |
| U-30 syringes | Draw BW into vial; allows precise low-volume draws | Medical supply or Amazon |
| Insulin syringes (U-100, 29–31g) | Draw and inject doses | Pharmacy (no Rx needed in most US states) |

### Storage

| Item | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Refrigerator | Store reconstituted vials (2–8°C / 36–46°F) |
| Sharps container | Safe needle disposal |

## Syringe Selection

**U-100 is the standard** for injecting peptides — 100 units per ml. This is what most dosing calculators assume, including the one in Step 4. U-100 syringes (29–31g, 1/2") are available at most pharmacies without a prescription.

---

Once you have your peptide vials and supplies on hand, store everything properly before proceeding.
