## Sourcing Peptides

Peptides exist in a regulatory gray area in most countries. They are sold as "for research use only, not for human consumption" — but they are widely used by individuals who self-administer.

Use a trusted vendor. While certification is difficult to verify independently, reputation in the community carries significant weight. Ask someone you trust for a referral before purchasing.

Beyond the peptide itself, you need the following before you can proceed safely.

### Other Supplies

- **Bacteriostatic water (BW)** — solvent for reconstitution; available at medical supply or compounding pharmacy
- **Alcohol swabs** — sterilize vial tops and injection sites; available at any pharmacy
- **Sharps container** — for safe needle disposal; available at any pharmacy

## Syringe Selection

**U-100 is the standard** for injecting peptides — 100 units per ml. This is what most dosing calculators assume, including the one in Step 4. U-100 syringes (29–31g, 1/2") are available at most pharmacies without a prescription.

## Storage

### Why It Matters

Peptides are fragile molecules. Exposure to heat, light, or moisture can break the peptide bonds and render them inactive — or in worst cases, produce degradation products. A peptide that has been stored improperly is money wasted and potentially a health risk.

### Before Reconstitution (Lyophilized Powder)

Unreconstituted powder in sealed vials is stable at refrigerator temperature. A cool, dry place also works for most peptides. Avoid heat, humidity, and direct light.

### After Reconstitution

Once you have added bacteriostatic water to a vial, storage rules change:

- **Always refrigerate** at 2–8°C (36–46°F)
- **Never freeze** a reconstituted vial — ice crystal formation damages the peptide
- **Typical stability** after reconstitution: 4–8 weeks for most peptides. When in doubt, use within 4 weeks and discard any unused portion
- **Protect from light** — store in a box or wrap in foil

Label each vial immediately after reconstituting with: peptide name, date of reconstitution, and concentration (mg/ml). Memory is unreliable when you have multiple vials.

### Signs a Peptide Has Degraded

Inspect your reconstituted vial before each use:

- **Normal**: clear, colorless to very slightly yellow solution, no particles
- **Discard if**: cloudy, discolored, visibly particulate, or smells unusual

<SafetyCallout variant="danger" title="When in Doubt, Throw It Out">
  A degraded or contaminated peptide vial is not worth the risk. Err on the side of discarding.
</SafetyCallout>

### Bacteriostatic Water

Bacteriostatic water (BW) contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol, which inhibits bacterial growth and extends the shelf life of reconstituted vials. Always use BW — never tap water, distilled water, or saline. Once opened, store BW refrigerated and use within 28 days.
