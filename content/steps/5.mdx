## Overview

Drawing is the process of pulling a precise volume of reconstituted peptide from the vial into your insulin syringe. Accuracy here directly affects your dose.

Many people find that drawing a week or more in advance does not change potency. If your schedule requires it, pre-drawing is a practical option.

## What You Need

- Reconstituted peptide vial (refrigerated)
- Insulin syringe (U-100, appropriate needle length for your draw volume)
- Your calculated syringe unit mark (from Step 4 calculator)

## Procedure

**1. Remove the vial from the refrigerator.**
Let it sit at room temperature for 5–10 minutes. Injecting cold liquid is uncomfortable and can cause local irritation.

**2. Wipe the vial rubber stopper with an alcohol swab.**
Let it air dry for 15–20 seconds.

**3. Pull air into your syringe equal to your draw volume.**
If you are drawing 10 units, pull 10 units of air into the syringe first. This creates positive pressure in the vial and makes drawing easier.

**4. Insert the needle into the vial and push the air in.**
Invert the vial so the needle points up (vial upside down). The needle tip should be submerged in the liquid.

**5. Draw back the plunger to your target unit mark.**
Pull slowly and steadily. If you overshoot, push a little back and re-draw.

**6. Remove the needle from the vial.**
Pull straight out without bending the needle.

**7. Verify your dose.**
Hold the syringe up to a light source and confirm you are at the correct unit mark.

<SafetyCallout variant="warning" title="Never Reuse Needles">
  Insulin needles are single-use. Reusing needles introduces contamination risk, causes microscopic barbing that increases injection pain, and can push skin cells or bacteria into your vial. Use a fresh needle for every injection.
</SafetyCallout>

---

With a loaded syringe, you are ready to inject.
