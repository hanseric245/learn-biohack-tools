## What This Guide Is

A practical, step-by-step walkthrough for self-administering research peptides safely — from choosing a peptide and sourcing supplies through reconstitution, injection technique, and disposal.

It is written for people who have decided to explore peptides and want to do it correctly.

## What Are Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the same building blocks as proteins. Many occur naturally in the body, playing roles in tissue repair, hormone signaling, and immune function. Most peptides in use are synthetic versions of these naturally occurring compounds.

## How to Use This Guide

Work through the steps in order. Each step builds on the previous one. Use the sidebar to navigate or pick up where you left off. The guide is designed to be read once end-to-end before your first injection.

<SafetyCallout variant="warning" title="A couple of things to know">
  **This guide is for educational purposes only.** Peptides are not approved for human use by the FDA. Self-administration carries real risks. Consult a qualified healthcare provider before beginning any peptide protocol.

  **If you have a history of cancer or elevated cancer risk:** Several peptides — particularly growth hormone secretagogues like CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, and GHRP variants — promote IGF-1 elevation, which is associated with increased cancer cell proliferation. Talk to an oncologist before using these compounds.
</SafetyCallout>
