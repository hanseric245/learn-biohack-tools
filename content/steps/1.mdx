## What Are Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the same building blocks that make up proteins. Many occur naturally in the body, and they play roles in signaling for repair, hormone regulation, and immune function. Most of the peptides in use are synthetic versions.

A significant population of individuals self-administers these compounds for purposes ranging from injury recovery to cognitive enhancement.

## Start With Your Goal

The most important question is: **what are you trying to accomplish?**

Different peptides are suited to different goals. Common goal categories include:

- **Injury recovery and tissue repair** — BPC-157, TB-500
- **Fat loss / Metabolic** — Retatrutide, AOD-9604
- **Muscle growth** — CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin, Tesamorelin
- **Cognitive function** — Semax, Selank, Cerebrolysin
- **Skin, hair, and anti-aging** — GHK-Cu, Epitalon
- **Immune modulation** — Thymosin Alpha-1, LL-37
- **Sexual health** — PT-141 (Bremelanotide), Melanotan 2, Kisspeptin

Be specific about your goal. "General wellness" is too vague to build a safe, purposeful protocol around.

## Research Before You Commit

Before choosing a peptide, do your own research from multiple sources:

1. **PubMed** — search for human or animal studies on the peptide you're considering
2. **Longecity and Reddit communities** (r/Peptides, r/NootropicsDepot) — real-world anecdotal reports
3. **Examine.com** — accessible summaries of research literature
4. **ClinicalTrials.gov** — to see what has been formally studied in humans

<SafetyCallout variant="info" title="Look for human data">
  Many peptides have solid animal study data but limited or no human trial evidence. Understand the gap between "worked in rodents" and "proven safe/effective in humans." This doesn't mean avoid them — it means calibrate your expectations and caution accordingly.
</SafetyCallout>

## Understand Peptide Forms

Peptides come in different forms depending on the peptide and vendor:

- **Lyophilized powder** — freeze-dried, requires reconstitution with bacteriostatic water before injection. Most injectable peptides come in this form.
- **Oral/sublingual** — a small number of peptides can be taken orally; however, bioavailability is usually much lower.
- **Nasal spray** — Semax and Selank are commonly taken intranasally.

This guide focuses on **injectable lyophilized peptides**, which require reconstitution and subcutaneous or intramuscular injection.

## Know the Contraindications

Before proceeding, be aware of general contraindications that apply across most peptides:

- Pregnancy or breastfeeding

<SafetyCallout variant="danger" title="Cancer Risk Signal">
  Several peptides, particularly growth hormone secretagogues (CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, GHRP variants), promote IGF-1 elevation. Elevated IGF-1 is associated with increased cancer cell proliferation. If you have any history of cancer or are at elevated cancer risk, consult an oncologist before using these compounds.
</SafetyCallout>

## Choosing Your First Peptide

For first-time users, simpler and better-tolerated peptides are a good starting point:

- **BPC-157** — well-tolerated, wide therapeutic window, studied in animals for gut, tendon, and nerve repair
- **TB-500** — tissue repair and recovery, well-tolerated

BPC-157 paired with TB-500 is a solid starting protocol — both are well-tolerated and complementary, and most people can benefit from that combination.

Avoid stacking multiple peptides until you understand how you respond to each individually. Introduce one at a time.
