## It Matters — Don't Put Them in the Trash

Used needles are a biohazard. Loose needles in household trash put sanitation workers and family members at risk of needlestick injury. Don't do it.

The good news: disposal is simple. **Check your local municipality for their specific rules on sharps disposal.** Rules vary by location. In Florida, a hard plastic milk or detergent bottle that is taped shut and clearly labeled is an acceptable last resort — but a dedicated sharps container is still the preferred option. [See Florida's guidelines for home sharps disposal.](https://www.floridahealth.gov/community-environmental-public-health/environmental-public-health/home-management-of-sharps/)

A dedicated sharps container (available at any pharmacy for a few dollars) is the cleanest option and accepted everywhere.

## Disposing of Expired or Unused Peptide Vials

When a vial reaches the end of its life:

- **Do not pour liquid down the drain** — bacteriostatic water with benzyl alcohol should not enter the water supply
- **Mix with an undesirable substance** (used coffee grounds, dirt) in a sealable bag, then place in household trash
- **Or use a medication take-back program** — most pharmacies accept liquids in original containers
