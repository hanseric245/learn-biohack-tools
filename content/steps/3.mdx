## What Is Reconstitution?

Lyophilized peptide powder is freeze-dried. It must be dissolved in bacteriostatic water before it can be injected. This process is called reconstitution, and you do it **once per vial** — not before every injection.

After reconstitution, the vial is stored in the refrigerator and you draw from it repeatedly until it is empty or expired.

## Reconstitution Procedure

### What You Need

- Your peptide vial (lyophilized powder)
- Bacteriostatic water (BW) vial
- One 18–21g reconstitution needle on a 1–3ml syringe
- Alcohol swabs
- Clean surface (paper towel or sterile field)

<SafetyCallout variant="warning" title="Work Clean">
  Wash your hands thoroughly before handling any vials or needles. If you have nitrile gloves, wear them. Work on a clean, flat surface. The biggest risk during reconstitution is contamination.
</SafetyCallout>

### Step-by-Step

**1. Wipe both vial tops with an alcohol swab.**
Let them air dry for 15–20 seconds. Do not blow on them or fan them dry.

**2. Draw the correct amount of bacteriostatic water into your reconstitution syringe.**
Use the amount from the calculator above. Be precise — the concentration your calculator result is based on this volume.

**3. Inject the BW slowly into the peptide vial.**
Insert the needle at an angle through the rubber stopper. Let the water run down the inside wall of the vial — do not squirt it directly onto the powder, as this can damage peptide structure.

**4. Do not shake the vial.**
Gentle swirling is fine. Vigorous shaking introduces air bubbles and can denature the peptide.

**5. Let it sit for 1–2 minutes.**
Most peptides dissolve quickly. Gently swirl again if needed. The solution should be clear when fully dissolved.

**6. Visually inspect the vial.**
The reconstituted solution should be clear and colorless to very faintly yellow. If it is cloudy, has visible particles, or has unusual color, do not use it.

**7. Label the vial immediately.**
Write: peptide name, date reconstituted, volume of BW used, concentration (mg/ml).

**8. Refrigerate.**
Your reconstituted vial goes in the fridge immediately after use. Do not leave it at room temperature.

## Understanding Concentration

Concentration (mg/ml) is what connects your dose to your draw volume:

```
Concentration = Vial amount (mg) ÷ BW added (ml)
Draw volume (ml) = Dose (mg) ÷ Concentration (mg/ml)
Syringe units = Draw volume (ml) × Syringe units per ml
```

**Example:**
- 10mg vial + 2ml BW → 5 mg/ml concentration
- Dose: 0.25mg (250 mcg)
- Draw volume: 0.25 ÷ 5 = 0.05ml
- On U-100 syringe (100 units/ml): 0.05 × 100 = **5 units**

The calculator handles this math. But understanding it helps you catch errors.

<SafetyCallout variant="danger" title="Double-Check Your Math">
  Dosing errors with peptides are usually concentration errors — using a different BW volume than assumed by your calculator, or confusing mcg and mg. Before your first injection from a newly reconstituted vial, verify the math manually or re-run the calculator with your actual BW amount.
</SafetyCallout>
