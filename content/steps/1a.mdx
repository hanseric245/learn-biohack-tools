## Why Storage Matters

Peptides are fragile molecules. Exposure to heat, light, moisture, or repeated freeze-thaw cycles can break the peptide bonds and render them inactive — or in worst cases, produce degradation products that could cause immune reactions.

Correct storage is not optional. A peptide that has been stored improperly is money wasted and potentially a health risk.

## Before Reconstitution (Lyophilized Powder)

Unreconstituted peptide powder in sealed vials is stable for longer periods, but still requires proper conditions:

| Condition | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Short-term (weeks to months) | Refrigerate at 2–8°C (36–46°F) |
| Long-term (months to years) | Freeze at -20°C (-4°F) |
| Light exposure | Store in original box or dark location |
| Humidity | Avoid storing in bathrooms or humid areas |

<SafetyCallout variant="warning" title="Avoid Repeated Freeze-Thaw Cycles">
  Each time a peptide is frozen and thawed, there is structural degradation risk. If you plan to store a vial long-term, freeze it immediately upon receipt. Only thaw when you are ready to reconstitute. Do not re-freeze after reconstitution.
</SafetyCallout>

## After Reconstitution

Once you have added bacteriostatic water to a vial, storage rules change:

- **Always refrigerate** at 2–8°C (36–46°F)
- **Never freeze** a reconstituted vial — ice crystal formation damages the peptide
- **Typical stability** after reconstitution: 4–8 weeks for most peptides, though this varies. When in doubt, use within 4 weeks and discard any unused portion
- **Protect from light** — consider wrapping vials in foil or storing in a box

### Labeling Your Vials

After reconstituting a vial, immediately label it with:

1. Peptide name
2. Date of reconstitution
3. Concentration (mg/ml) — see Step 3 for how to calculate this
4. Amount of bacteriostatic water used

This is not optional. Memory is unreliable, especially when you have multiple vials.

## Signs a Peptide Has Degraded

Inspect your reconstituted vial before each use:

- **Normal**: clear, colorless to very slightly yellow solution, no particles
- **Discard if**: cloudy, discolored, visibly particulate, or smells unusual
- **Discard if**: the vial was left unrefrigerated for more than a few hours

<SafetyCallout variant="danger" title="When in Doubt, Throw It Out">
  A degraded or contaminated peptide vial is not worth the risk of injection site infection, systemic illness, or unpredictable biological response. Peptides are not expensive relative to the cost of a medical visit. Err on the side of discarding.
</SafetyCallout>

## Bacteriostatic Water

Bacteriostatic water (BW) contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol, which inhibits bacterial growth and gives reconstituted vials a longer shelf life than plain sterile water. Always use BW for reconstitution — never tap water, distilled water, or saline (unless a specific peptide requires it).

BW itself has a shelf life. Once opened:
- Store refrigerated
- Use within 28 days (standard guideline from USP)
- Discard if cloudy or particulate
